Research

  • Dissertation: My project is a feasibility study of creating online tutoring services for students enrolled in the technical writing service course. Blending user experience and service design methodologies, I surveyed and interviewed key stakeholders in order to design a digital studio that offers resources and services that best meet their needs. The 360 Digital Studio is opening as a pilot study in Fall 2018 with myself as coordinator, another graduate student as assistant coordinator, and three undergraduate Professional & Technical Writing majors as interns.

  • Writing center mission statements: Using Cheryl Geisler's (2004) systematic coding approach to verbal data, I coded and analyzed 100 mission statements from writing centers in the United States. After an initial round of analysis focusing on the representation of writing center work on websites, I developed this research question: What kinds of claims do writing centers in the United States make about the benefits of their work through mission statements posted on their websites?

    I used a criterion method to extract sections of mission statements into a spreadsheet that were then segmented for coding. Seven benefits of writing centers emerged from the data: epistemological, critical thinking, independence, success, research, writers, and supporting the institution. The inter-rater reliability is 90%, and the Cohen's Kappa calculation is considered good at 0.722. The quantitative results of this analysis are as follows:

    Benefit of Writing Center Number (n=100)
    Epistemological 24
    Critical Thinking 15
    Independence 44
    Success 21
    Research 13
    Writers 63
    Support Institution 16

Projects My Students Produced

Science Writing students collaboratively created a website highlighting the concepts and skills they felt other science communicators should know. I recoded this website using Bootstrap and GitHub to give the site a different design and include some of the design requests students had but couldn't accomplish in WordPress (such as an accordion feature on the Spotlight Interviews page). In addition, students collaborated with Virginia Tech's Center for Communicating Science and the Fralin Life Science Institute to interview and write science stories about STEM graduate students who are female and/or people of color.
